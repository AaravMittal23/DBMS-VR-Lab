Experiment: Database Normalization Using Functional Dependencies

SRM Institute of Science and Technology
Department of Computer Science
Post-Test

Answer the following questions to evaluate your understanding after completing the experiment.

1. In a relation R(A, B, C) with FDs: A → B and B → C, what normal form is it in after normalizing to 3NF?

2. What is the main difference between 3NF and BCNF?

3. In the relation Order(order_id, product_id, product_name, quantity), if order_id, product_id is the primary key and product_id → product_name, what normal form is it in?

4. What is lossless decomposition?

5. In a relation with composite primary key (A, B), if attribute C depends only on A, what type of dependency is this?

6. What is a trivial functional dependency?

7. When normalizing a relation, what should be preserved?

8. In the relation Student(student_id, name, dept_id, dept_name), if student_id → dept_id and dept_id → dept_name, what normal form is it in?

9. What is the primary reason for normalizing a database beyond 3NF to BCNF?

10. In a relation R(A, B, C, D) with FDs: A → B, B → C, and A, D → C, what is a candidate key?

11. What happens when you normalize a table that is already in 3NF to BCNF?

12. What is dependency preservation in normalization?

13. In normalization, what is the trade-off between higher normal forms?

14. What is the minimum normal form required to eliminate insertion anomalies?

15. When should you consider denormalization?
