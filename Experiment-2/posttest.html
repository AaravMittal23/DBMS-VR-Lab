Post-Test

Answer the following questions to evaluate your understanding after completing the experiment.

1. When mapping a weak entity to a relational schema, what must be included in the relation?

2. In a one-to-one relationship, where should the foreign key be placed?

3. What is the primary key of a relation created from a many-to-many relationship?

4. How should a composite attribute be handled when mapping to a relational schema?

5. What does total participation (double line) in an ER diagram indicate?

6. In an ER diagram, how is a weak entity typically represented?

7. What is the difference between a strong entity and a weak entity?

8. When mapping a multi-valued attribute to a relational schema, what approach is typically used?

9. What is the purpose of an identifying relationship in an ER diagram?

10. In a one-to-many relationship, where is the foreign key placed in the relational schema?

11. What does the notation "1:N" represent in an ER diagram?

12. How are derived attributes typically handled in a relational schema?

13. What is the first step in mapping an ER diagram to a relational schema?

14. In an ER diagram, what does a double line connecting an entity to a relationship indicate?

15. What is the main purpose of mapping an ER diagram to a relational schema?
