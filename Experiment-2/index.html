Introduction

Welcome to the Database Management Systems Virtual Laboratory. This virtual lab is designed to help students understand and practice the Design of Entity–Relationship (ER) Diagrams and Relational Mapping. Through this interactive platform, you will learn how to model real-world scenarios using ER diagrams, identify entities, attributes, and relationships, and convert conceptual ER models into normalized relational database schemas.

The main objective of this virtual lab is to provide hands-on experience with ER modeling and relational mapping, enabling students to visualize and understand how conceptual database designs are transformed into implementable relational schemas. This platform combines theoretical knowledge with practical implementation, helping you acquire practical learning experiences in database design and modeling.

Learning Outcomes

After completing this virtual lab experiment, you will be able to:

  • Design ER diagrams for given problem domains using standard ER notation
  • Identify and represent entities, attributes, and relationships correctly
  • Apply cardinality and participation constraints appropriately
  • Map ER diagrams to relational schemas following standard mapping rules
  • Create relational tables with proper primary keys, foreign keys, and constraints
  • Handle complex scenarios including weak entities, composite attributes, and relationship attributes
  • Understand the relationship between conceptual design (ER model) and logical design (relational model)
